FAQs

Answers to the most common questions about the work.

About the Work

The Yurodivian is a network built around a series of books on inner development — Soul Path. It exists to support people who are taking seriously the question of how to live, and who want something more grounded than motivation and more honest than most of what passes for spirituality.

The yurodiviy — holy fool — is a figure from the Eastern Christian tradition. Unlike conventional saints, they operated outside the agreed rules of society, speaking uncomfortable truths and refusing to perform respectability. They were not mad. They had simply stepped outside the shared agreement we call sanity, and from that position they could see clearly. This network takes its name from that impulse.

No. There is no doctrine, no membership, no hierarchy of belief. The work draws on ideas from many traditions without belonging to any of them. You are not asked to believe anything — only to apply the practices and observe what happens.

Anyone who has the sense that there is more available to them than what they are currently living — and who is willing to do something about it. The work is not for people who want to feel good about themselves. It is for people who want to be honest with themselves.

Soul Path & Books

Soul Embodiment is the first volume in the Soul Path series. It is a foundational guide to understanding your soul's blueprint and beginning the work of living in alignment with it. The book contains both a framework for understanding and practical exercises for applying what you read. It is available to download for free.

The Soul Path series is planned as three volumes. Book 1, Soul Embodiment, is available now. Book 2, Book of Power, and Book 3, Book of Wisdom, are in development. Updates on their progress are posted in the Updates section.

Yes. Each volume builds on the one before it. Soul Embodiment establishes the foundation that the later books assume. Beginning elsewhere will not give you what you are looking for.

Community

Meetings are periodic video gatherings where members share their experiences with the material, ask questions, and discuss the work together. Recordings of past meetings are available in the Meetings section. They are not lectures — the format is open and conversational.

Stories are first-person accounts submitted by readers describing what has shifted in their lives since working with the material. They are tagged by how long the person has been engaged with the work and by the area of life that changed. They are not endorsements — they are honest accounts, including the complicated ones.

Use the contact form. Write plainly and honestly — the simpler the better. Stories that read like testimonials are not published. Stories that read like journal entries often are.
