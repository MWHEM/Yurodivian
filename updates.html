The Fool's Gold: On Sacred Madness in the Modern World

There is a peculiar wisdom that cannot be taught, only uncovered — a knowing that arrives not through study but through surrender. The yurodiviy, the holy fool of the Eastern tradition, walked among the powerful and the broken alike, speaking truths that no other could say. They were not mad. They had simply stepped outside the shared agreement we call sanity, and from that vantage point, they could see everything.

In this first transmission, we explore what it means to carry that impulse in a modern life — and why the world may need holy foolishness now more than ever.
